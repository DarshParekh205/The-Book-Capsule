The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald

The Great Gatsby is a novel about the American Dream, set in the Jazz Age, centered around Jay Gatsby and his infatuation with Daisy Buchanan. Narrated by Nick Carraway, it explores themes of love, wealth, and the American dream’s hollowness.

Key Takeaways:

  • 1. The Illusion of the American Dream: Gatsby’s pursuit of wealth and status ultimately proves hollow.
  • 2. The Power of Love and Obsession: Gatsby’s obsession with Daisy reflects the dangers of idealized love.
  • 3. Social Class and Identity: The novel critiques social stratification and the inability to transcend one’s past.

This book is a classic critique of the American Dream, making it a must-read for those interested in social commentary, historical fiction, and romance.
