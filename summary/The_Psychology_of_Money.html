The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel

The Psychology of Money explores the emotional and behavioral side of managing money. Morgan Housel shares 19 timeless lessons on wealth, greed, and happiness, emphasizing how personal behavior often matters more than financial theory.

Key Takeaways:

  • 1. Behavior > Knowledge: Managing money isn’t about intelligence—it's about behavior and discipline.
  • 2. Wealth is What You Don’t See: True wealth is built by saving and investing, not by spending for appearances.
  • 3. Compounding is the Magic: Small, consistent efforts over time build massive results—like Warren Buffett’s fortune.

This book teaches that financial success is less about spreadsheets and more about self-awareness, patience, and long-term thinking. It's a must-read for building a healthier relationship with money.
