Noise: A Flaw in Human Judgment

Noise uncovers an invisible but pervasive problem in decision-making: random variability, or “noise.” While bias is consistent error, noise is inconsistent error, and it’s just as harmful — especially in areas like law, healthcare, hiring, and predictions.

Key Takeaways:

  • 1. What is Noise: Noise is unwanted variability in judgments — two experts may give wildly different answers to the same problem.
  • 2. Bias vs. Noise: Bias pushes decisions in a specific direction; noise scatters them unpredictably. Both distort judgment.
  • 3. Noise Audit: Organizations should perform audits to measure judgment variability among professionals.

This book is essential for professionals and leaders who want to improve fairness, accuracy, and quality in decision-making. It helps uncover flaws that often go unnoticed and provides actionable solutions to tackle them.
